Publications
"A Virus Has No Religion": Analyzing Islamophobia on Twitter During the COVID-19 Outbreak
Mohit Chandra*, Manvith Reddy*, Shradha Sehgal*, Saurabh Gupta, Arun Balaji Buduru, Ponnurangam Kumaraguru
ACM Hypertext, 2021
doi / code

We study the rise in Islamophobia on Twitter in association with COVID-19. We explore the topics of discussion, toxicity of external URLs, semantic changes of words, and the correlation of online and offline events. We make public the CoronaBias dataset with 410,990 tweets from 244,229 unique users, across Feb-May'21.

What's Kooking? Characterizing India's Emerging Social Network, Koo
Asmit Kumar Singh*, Chirag Jain*, Jivitesh Jain*, Rishi Raj Jain*, Shradha Sehgal* , Tanisha Pandey*, Ponnurangam Kumaraguru
ASONAM, 2021 (Best Student Paper)
arXiv / blog / video (english) / video (hindi) / video (telugu)

We characterize the emerging multilingual Indian microblogging app, Koo. We collect a dataset of 4.07 million users, 163.12 million follower-following relationships, and their content and activity across 12 languages. We study the linguistic communities, user demographic, prominent accounts, activity, and more. Our characterization highlights the dynamics of the multilingual social network and its diverse Indian user base.

Knowledge-based Neural Framework for Sexism Detection and Classification
Harika Abburi, Shradha Sehgal, Himanshu Maheshwari, Vasudeva Varma
IberLEF@SEPLN, 2021
paper / code

We propose a model for sexism detection and fine-grained classification in Twitter and Gab posts. The proposed neural model combines tweet representations obtained using the RoBERTa model and linguistic features such as Empath, Hurtlex, and Perspective API, by involving recurrent components.

Capitol (Pat)riots: A comparative study of Twitter and Parler
Hitkul, Avinash Prabhu*, Dipanwita Guhathakurta*, Jivitesh Jain*, Mallika Subramanian*, Manvith Reddy*, Shradha Sehgal*, Tanvi Karandikar*, Amogh Gulati*, Udit Arora*, Rajiv Ratn Shah, Ponnurangam Kumaraguru
ArXiv, 2021
arXiv / infographic

We compare the role of Parler and Twitter in the Capitol Riots that took place on Jan 6,2021. We collected data from both platforms based on the trending hashtags and drew comparisons based on what the topics of discussions, trending accounts, and organic nature of the content generated. While the content trending on Twitter had strong resentments towards the event and called for action against rioters and inciters, Parler content had a conservative narrative echoing the ideas of voter fraud similar to the attacking mob.
